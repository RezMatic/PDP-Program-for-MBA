WHY YOU NEED COMMUNICATION SKILL?

Effective communication skills are paramount for business students to thrive in professional settings. Mastery of clear and articulate verbal communication enables them to convey ideas persuasively in meetings, presentations, and negotiations. Proficiency in written communication ensures the clarity and professionalism of emails, reports, and other written correspondence. Business students should also excel in adapting their communication style to diverse audiences, whether clients, colleagues, or stakeholders, fostering understanding and engagement. Ultimately, honing communication skills equips business students with the ability to convey their ideas effectively, build strong relationships, and drive success in their careers.

WHY YOU NEED WORKPLACE READINESS SKILL?

Workplace Readiness Skill is preparing a new workplace entrant (like a student like you) to enter the workforce with the requisite knowledge, skill, abilities and attributes to perform the job efficiently at the workplace. These are the skills and knowledge which will develop abilities in you to perform the job professionally. These skills which you will learn will develop technical competencies which are specific to your job and area of speacialization like HR, Marketing, IT etc. You are a student with a robust dream. Think about knowing how to start the work in your office before you have been in your office. Workplace readiness will give an added advantage and as wisely said "He who sweats more in training bleeds less in battle".

WHAT IS CAPSTONE PROJECT?

Capstone projects for business students serve as the culmination of their academic journey, integrating theoretical knowledge with real-world application. These projects typically involve in-depth research, strategic analysis, and practical problem-solving, offering students a platform to showcase their skills and creativity. Through capstone projects, students tackle complex business challenges, develop innovative solutions, and demonstrate their readiness for the professional world. They often collaborate with industry partners or work on consulting projects, gaining valuable insights and networking opportunities. Capstone projects foster critical thinking, leadership, and teamwork, preparing students to make meaningful contributions to the business community.

They provide a space for students to apply various business theories, methodologies, and tools to address contemporary issues, fostering both personal and professional growth. In essence, capstone projects empower business students to bridge the gap between academia and the corporate realm, fostering innovation and excellence.

Learn about the benefits of agglomeration and the effect it has on real estate prices.

  • Recognise the forces that drive firms and residents to locate in urban areas
  • Identify the productivity benefits of operating in larger and denser cities
  • Articulate why central locations have higher real estate prices
  • Determine whether or not cities are monocentric
  • Determine where commerce, individuals, and manufacturers will locate based on the bid rent model

Discover what drives supply and demand in real estate markets and how this determines prices and trends.

  • Demonstrate an understanding of the concepts of supply, demand, and their elasticity
  • Apply the basic concepts of supply and demand to practical scenarios
  • Discuss whether higher property prices are considered normatively positive or normatively negative
  • Explain why property prices increase faster in places where supply is constrained
  • Determine the demand- and supply-side factors that contribute to differences in property prices

Discover the different stages of the real estate cycle.

  • Recognise that exogenous real estate cycles are driven by demand and therefore resemble business cycles
  • Discuss what influences supply and demand for real estate in the short term
  • Recall the stages of the real estate cycle and their characteristics
  • Identify the endogenous factors that affect real estate cycles
  • Determine the stage of the real estate cycle that the market is in

Understand that a building is a bundle of features that can be priced separately.

  • Recognise that a number of features can be used to characterise different properties
  • Discuss how the hedonic model can be applied to valuing non-market goods
  • Outline the advantages and shortcomings of the hedonic model
  • Show how the hedonic-model approach can be used to price different features of a property
  • Conclude which property to purchase using hedonic modelling to assist in the decision-making process

Determine the value of a property based on the stream of income it creates.

  • Discuss the different types of properties that produce rental income
  • Outline the rationale behind purchasing the right to not pay rent
  • Apply basic concepts of financial mathematics to value assets
  • Calculate the value of a property using a stream of incomes

Determine the best way to finance property with collateralised debt.

  • Recognise the usefulness of a mortgage as a source of capital and a multiplier of returns
  • Identify the components that comprise the weighted average cost of capital
  • Apply the concept of the weighted average cost of capital
  • Identify the different types of fixed-rate mortgages
  • Discuss mortgage refinancing options
  • Calculate the difference in returns between financing options
  • Decide which mortgage provides the better value for the purchase of a property

Learn about using real estate to diversify a portfolio.

  • Outline the different asset classes and the concept of diversification
  • Identify the different vehicles for investing in the real estate asset class
  • Choose asset classes that should be included in a portfolio
  • Describe the characteristics of real estate investment trusts
  • Articulate the role of real estate investment trusts in diversifying a portfolio
  • Compare the returns of a diversified and non-diversified portfolio of assets

Learn how sustainability can contribute to investment returns.

  • Highlight why sustainability is important in a real estate context
  • Discuss local sustainability initiatives and ways to incorporate sustainability into real estate practices
  • Outline the costs and benefits of making properties more sustainable
  • Calculate the impact of sustainability practices on investment properties of real assets
  • Recommend which property investment to make in order to maximise returns
  • Recognise what you have achieved during this course
